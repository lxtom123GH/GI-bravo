Help & Guide

See it in action

New here? Watch a simulated roast — no microphone or beans needed — or take a quick guided tour of the screen.

First-time setup

  1. Use Chrome or Edge (needed for the microphone and Bluetooth).
  2. Install the app (optional): address-bar install icon, or Share → Add to Home Screen on a phone. It then works offline.
  3. Allow the microphone when asked (first time you start a roast).
  4. Pick your Mode (bottom of the sidebar): Easy, Moderate (recommended), or Expert.
  5. Add beans in Bean Pantry, then set your roaster on Active Roast.
  6. Optional: tap Calibrate Noise and stay quiet for 3 s to improve crack detection.

Doing a roast

  1. On Active Roast, choose the beans and roaster settings.
  2. Optional: pick a Follow reference roast to overlay a past curve with pre-crack cues.
  3. Tap Start Roast & Listening and allow the microphone — cracks are detected automatically (beep + timeline), and you can also press Manual: Mark and log power changes (P5 → P3) during the roast. If the mic won't work, tap Start (no mic) to run the timer and mark everything yourself.
  4. On iPhone/iPad: open the app in Safari (the browser) and allow the microphone when asked. Mic access can be unreliable when launched from a home-screen icon — if Start can't get the mic, the status bar tells you why, and Start (no mic) always works.
  5. Watch the live timer and DTR. (Expert) log bean temps for Rate of Rise, or connect a Bluetooth probe.
  6. Tap Stop Roast to drop — it's saved to history automatically.

📱 The app keeps your screen awake during a roast so crack detection keeps running. Leave it on this screen — if you manually lock the phone or switch to another app, listening pauses until you come back.

After the roast

In Roast History: Log Roasted Weight for weight-loss %, Edit Notes for tasting/cupping, add a colour photo to track how dark each batch is (see below), and Export a summary or CSV.

📷 Roast-colour photos

Photos let you compare how dark your roasts are over time. Because room lighting changes the colours your camera sees, the app can colour-correct a photo so the reading is comparable batch to batch. Pick the option that suits you — each gives a roast-colour number (lower = darker):

  • Add Photo — just a picture, no correction. Fine for a quick visual record.
  • Add Colour-Corrected Photo — easiest and recommended. Under the same light, photograph a plain grey or white card, then the beans. That's it. A neutral grey card works best.
  • Add ColorChecker Photo — most accurate. If you happen to own a 24-patch ColorChecker card, photograph it with the beans and tap its 4 corners. Overkill for most home roasters.
  • Add Custom-Target Photo — a budget DIY version of the ColorChecker (see how-to below).
Make a cheap DIY colour target

Stick 4–6 paint swatches on a card — a grey ramp (white → light grey → mid grey → near-black) is most reliable; add a warm and a cool chip for better colour. Then, under Add Custom-Target Photo:

  1. Choose “＋ Calibrate new target…”, name it, set the grid (columns × rows), photograph the card in good daylight, and tap the 4 corner patches. You only do this once — it saves the card's colours as a baseline.
  2. After that, pick your saved target, re-photograph the card next to your beans in whatever light you have, tap the 4 corners, add the beans photo, and press Process.

The reading is relative but repeatable — great for comparing your own roasts (it isn't an official Agtron number).

Tip: turn on 💡 Show hints (bottom-left) and tap the ⓘ next to any button for a quick explanation.

Ongoing use

  • Reproduce a roast: save it as a Behmor template, or follow it as a reference.
  • Compare two batches and view Trends over time in Roast History.
  • Back up regularly: Roast History → Data Backup → Export. Your data lives only in this browser, so keep backups.

Troubleshooting

  • “Start” seems to do nothing / mic error: the status bar now shows the reason. On iPhone/iPad use Safari and allow the mic (not the home-screen icon); on desktop use Chrome/Edge over the https link. Either way, Start (no mic) lets you roast without auto-detection.
  • Too many/few cracks: adjust Detection Settings → sensitivity and calibrate noise.
  • Screen dims or roast seems to pause: the app keeps the screen awake automatically, but if you locked the phone or switched apps, detection pauses. Stay on the roast screen; use Manual: Mark for any crack missed while away.
  • Data gone: it's per-browser and cleared if you wipe browsing data — keep backups.
