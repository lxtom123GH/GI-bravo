Help & Guide

See it in action

New here? Watch a simulated roast — no microphone or beans needed — or take a quick guided tour of the screen.

First-time setup

  1. Use Chrome or Edge (needed for the microphone and Bluetooth).
  2. Install the app (optional): address-bar install icon, or Share → Add to Home Screen on a phone. It then works offline.
  3. Allow the microphone when asked (first time you start a roast).
  4. Pick your Mode (sidebar → ⚙ Preferences): Easy, Moderate (recommended), or Expert.
  5. Your roaster: on Active Roast it shows your machine — most people use one, so there's nothing to set up. If you use more than one (yours, a friend's, a different Behmor/KKTO), tap ⚙ Manage roasters to add them; each roast is then tagged with the machine it was done on.
  6. Add beans in Bean Pantry — just a name and grams is enough; tap ＋ Add detail for optional origin/process/cost (and ⚙ Customise fields there hides the optional fields you don't track). Or use 📷 Quick-add from receipt to snap a receipt and add several at once. Then weigh out a batch and roast on Active Roast.
  7. Adding beans you've bought before is quick: the supplier, country, region and farm boxes suggest what you've typed before — and they work together. Pick a supplier and the countries you usually buy from it move to the top (and fill in if there's only one); pick a country and its usual region/process follow. Typing or pasting a full name like "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe Washed" fills in country, region and process for you.
  8. Noise calibration is automatic: once the mic is permitted (after your first roast), the app quietly measures the room while you set up — a 🎙 note shows when it's listening — and locks the level in when you start, so it hears your roaster warming up. Prefer manual? Detection Settings → Calibrate now.

Doing a roast

  1. On Active Roast, choose the beans and roaster settings. For the green weight, type it in or tap −5 / +5 to nudge to the exact grams (from empty the first tap seeds your usual weight, e.g. 420 g).
  2. Optional: pick a Follow reference roast to overlay a past curve with pre-crack cues.
  3. Tap Start Roast & Listening and allow the microphone — cracks are detected automatically (beep + timeline), and you can also press Manual: Mark and log power changes (P5 → P3) during the roast. If the mic won't work, tap Start (no mic) to run the timer and mark everything yourself.
  4. On iPhone/iPad: open the app in Safari (the browser) and allow the microphone when asked. Mic access can be unreliable when launched from a home-screen icon — if Start can't get the mic, the status bar tells you why, and Start (no mic) always works.
  5. New to your roaster? The 🎛️ control panel guides it: for a Behmor it shows what each button does before vs during a roast (pick your model — they differ); for a KKTO it explains heat/airflow and the roast-phase flow. During a roast it logs what you change onto the timeline.
  6. While roasting, the screen simplifies: setup and options tuck away, leaving a big timer, a phase strip (Drying → Maillard → Development), the crack buttons (mark, plus a ✗ clear that appears only after a crack is recorded, so there's nothing extra to hunt for beforehand), power logging and Stop Roast. Everything comes back when the roast ends.
  7. Watch the live timer and DTR. (Expert) log bean temps for Rate of Rise, or connect a Bluetooth probe.
  8. Tap Stop Roast to drop — it's saved to history automatically, and a Roast saved card shows your times, lets you enter the roasted weight right there, and jumps to notes/photos.

📱 The app keeps your screen awake during a roast so crack detection keeps running. Leave it on this screen — if you manually lock the phone or switch to another app, listening pauses until you come back.

After the roast

Enter the roasted weight on the Roast saved card (weight-loss % is worked out for you), then use its Add notes & photos button — or open Roast History yourself: ↻ Roast again sets up a repeat of any roast in one tap, Log Roasted Weight for weight-loss %, Edit Notes for tasting/cupping, add a colour photo to track how dark each batch is (see below), and Export a summary or CSV. The Best value card ranks your roasts by cup quality per dollar (tasting score ÷ cost per cup). In Edit Notes, the Tasting date lets you record how a coffee changes over time — taste again later and save with a new date.

📷 Roast-colour photos

Photos let you compare how dark your roasts are over time. Because room lighting changes the colours your camera sees, the app can colour-correct a photo so the reading is comparable batch to batch. Pick the option that suits you — each gives a roast-colour number (lower = darker):

  • Add Photo — just a picture, no correction. Fine for a quick visual record.
  • Add Colour-Corrected Photo — easiest and recommended. Under the same light, photograph a plain grey or white card, then the beans. That's it. A neutral grey card works best.
  • Add ColorChecker Photo — most accurate. If you happen to own a 24-patch ColorChecker card, photograph it with the beans and tap its 4 corners. Overkill for most home roasters.
  • Add Custom-Target Photo — a budget DIY version of the ColorChecker (see how-to below).
Make a cheap DIY colour target

Stick 4–6 paint swatches on a card — a grey ramp (white → light grey → mid grey → near-black) is most reliable; add a warm and a cool chip for better colour. Then, under Add Custom-Target Photo:

  1. Choose “＋ Calibrate new target…”, name it, set the grid (columns × rows), photograph the card in good daylight, and tap the 4 corner patches. You only do this once — it saves the card's colours as a baseline.
  2. After that, pick your saved target, re-photograph the card next to your beans in whatever light you have, tap the 4 corners, add the beans photo, and press Process.

The reading is relative but repeatable — great for comparing your own roasts (it isn't an official Agtron number).

Tip: turn on 💡 Show hints (sidebar → ⚙ Preferences) and tap the ⓘ next to any button for a quick explanation.

Ongoing use

  • Reproduce a roast: tap ↻ Roast again on it in Roast History (loads its bean and settings), save it as a Behmor template, or follow it as a reference.
  • Freshness: the Bean Pantry shows each bean's age, flags old lots, and marks the oldest to roast first. Each roast shows a 🌱 resting → ready (peak) → past peak badge.
  • Lots: bought another batch of the same bean? Tap ＋ Lot — grams is enough, or add a date/price/best-before. On-hand grams is the sum of lots, cost is the weighted average, and the Lots list shows which batch to use first. Roasting draws from the oldest lot first. Beans without lots stay simple.
  • Roasted stock: the Bean Pantry's Roasted stock section lists coffee you've roasted and still have — grams left and how long it's been resting, oldest first. Record a roast's roasted weight in Roast History to add it, then tap Drank some / Finished as you brew it down — log where it went (brewed / gift / cupping) and each batch keeps a little Where it went history.
  • Source & price: tap Source on a bean to see where you buy it, its price history (built from your dated lots) and whether the price is trending up or down — and ↗ Re-order opens the supplier's page in one tap. Add the supplier when you add the bean, or fill it in here.
  • Loans (borrowed / lent beans): swapped beans with a friend? Tap Loan on a bean to record "borrowed from Mark — owe 250 g" or "lent Sam 200 g". The card shows what you owe and have lent out, with a Loans list you can tidy up as beans come back.
  • Plan roasts: on a pantry bean, Plan roasts suggests sizes that fit your drum and divide a bag evenly (2.5 kg → 6 × 417 g, no runt).
  • Blends: in Bean Pantry → Blends, set ratios (e.g. 60/40), then Weigh out splits a batch into per-bean prep batches. Blends you can make now suggests classic recipes from the beans you have in stock — tap Use this recipe to pre-fill the builder. Set each bean's optional density/size and a pre-blend warns if beans won't roast evenly together.
  • Compare two batches and view Trends over time in Roast History.
  • Back up regularly: Roast History → Data Backup → Export. Your data lives only in this browser, so keep backups.
  • Sync across devices (optional): tap ☁️ Sign in to back up at the bottom of the sidebar to create an account and sync your pantry & roasts. It's entirely optional — the app works fully without it — and you can share a pantry with someone by email.

Troubleshooting

  • “Start” seems to do nothing / mic error: the status bar now shows the reason. On iPhone/iPad use Safari and allow the mic (not the home-screen icon); on desktop use Chrome/Edge over the https link. Either way, Start (no mic) lets you roast without auto-detection.
  • Too many/few cracks: adjust Detection Settings → sensitivity. Auto-calibration handles the noise floor while you set up (or tap Calibrate now there for a manual sample). Or turn on Auto-tune from my corrections there — then just clear any false crack (✗) and use Manual: Mark for any it misses, and the app quietly tunes the sensitivity for that roaster over time.
  • 🧪 Experimental — MFCC features: Detection Settings has an optional Compute MFCC features toggle (off by default). It computes the audio's timbre fingerprint alongside detection as groundwork for a future, more accurate crack detector — it doesn't change detection today. Safe to leave off.
  • 🧪 Roast Lab — capture & compare roasts: Detection Settings has a Roast Lab toggle (off by default). When on, each roast records a timeline of what the detector hears — loudness, crack pitch and the MFCC fingerprint — plus every crack and cleared false-alarm. It doesn't change detection. Afterwards, use Export JSON / CSV (or Copy summary) to save and compare roasts — or on a phone/tablet tap 📤 Share capture to send it straight to yourself (Mail / AirDrop / Save to Files), which is the easiest way to collect captures from several devices after the same roast. Handy for an A/B weekend: roast 1 baseline, roast 2 watch the numbers, roast 3 tweak the sensitivity and compare. Drop the exported file into the repo's roast-logs/ folder to analyse it later. If you're signed in, ticking ☁️ Back up captures to cloud (off by default) backs each capture up to your account so they collect automatically across your devices. For a step-by-step test session (e.g. a few roasts in a row), see ROAST_TEST_PROTOCOL.md in the repo.
  • 🫥 Shadow detectors (inside Roast Lab): while Roast Lab is on, a small bank of experimental crack detectors runs alongside the real one — each tuned differently (more/less sensitive, stricter 2C pitch, tighter clustering). They quietly log what they would have called, tagged by variant, into the same capture. They never alarm and never change your detection — they exist purely so the export can compare each variant against the real detector and your own crack/✗ marks. That comparison is how a smarter detector gets chosen safely, without ever trusting an unproven one mid-roast. Tip for testing: run the same roast on a couple of devices/positions and export each — more captures, same beans.
  • Screen dims or roast seems to pause: the app keeps the screen awake automatically, but if you locked the phone or switched apps, detection pauses. Stay on the roast screen; use Manual: Mark for any crack missed while away.
  • Data gone: it's per-browser and cleared if you wipe browsing data — keep backups.
