GI-Bravo DIY Colour Target (A4)

A printable frame for a home-made colour-calibration target. The geometry is printed (so the app can find it reliably under any light); the colour comes from paint sample chips you tape into the slots (stable, matte, far better than printer ink).

  1. Print at 100% scale (no "fit to page" / "shrink to fit"), A4, portrait. In the print dialog turn ON “Background graphics” so the black frame prints.
  2. Check the scale ruler at the bottom reads 100 mm with a real ruler. If not, reprint at 100%.
  3. Tape a paint chip into each numbered slot, matching the role label (white → near-black greys, three browns, plus orange / yellow / blue / green). Matte chips only — gloss causes glare. It's fine to leave a slot empty for now.
  4. In good, even daylight, photograph the whole sheet with a small single layer of your beans in the centre circle. Keep the sheet flat and fill the frame.
  5. In the app: calibrate the target once (it measures your chips), then re-shoot sheet + beans any time to colour-correct that batch.

This gives a repeatable, relative colour reading for comparing your own roasts over time — not an absolute Agtron number.
